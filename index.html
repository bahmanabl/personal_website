About Me

I’m currently a Presidential Postdoctoral Fellow at Virginia Tech, where I’m collaborating with the Link + License + Launch department to commercialize a GPU-based ultra-fast scheduler. We’ve secured a proof-of-concept grant and are actively working toward launching a startup around this technology.

As a researcher, I work at the intersection of machine learning and wireless communications. My recent projects include using reinforcement learning to counteract jamming attacks, optimizing URLLC/eMBB multiplexing strategies, and reducing the cost of LLM APIs through efficient caching techniques.

I’m passionate about building practical systems grounded in solid theoretical foundations.

Projects

  • Ultrafast Scheduler for 5G/NextG: A GPU-accelerated scheduler for real-time radio networks.
  • RL-based URLLC/eMBB Puncturing: Reinforcement learning for traffic multiplexing in 5G systems.
  • Multi Agent Reinforcement Learning for Cobatting ammer and Interference over the wireless edge: We developed off-policy and on-policy approached and compared their advantages and disadvantages.
